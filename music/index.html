Music

During my freshman year at Northwestern (2013), I became interested in digital music production.

I purchased a MIDI device, the M-Audio Keystation Mini 32, because it was the cheapest and smallest one I could find, and it came with a simple software called Ignite, which I used to make loops. I was gifted a copy of Sony ACID Music studio (a Digital Audio Workstation) and developed a process where after creating instrumental loops in Ignite, I would open them in ACID to polish them into a full piece. None of the below songs contain any kind of sampling, and each loop of each instrument is original, created on my Mini 32.

All this being said, haven’t spent much time making music since 2015, but a few of them are pretty decent, and I thought it would be fun to include them here.

None of them are named… It is what it is.
