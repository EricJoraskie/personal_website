About Me

My name is Eric Joraskie and I am a computer science graduate student at Northeastern University.

I’ve had an unusual path into CS. Prior to a string of injuries resulting in my football career prematurely coming to an end, I thought I’d end up playing in the NFL. Upon medically retiring from football toward the end of my sophomore year at Northwestern University, I began to focus my studies on neuroscience, which I found to be exciting enough to keep my attention. Falling in love with neuroscience led me to contemplate both the pursuit of a PhD in neuroscience and medical school at different points throughout my senior year, but neither path was tantalizing enough for me to take the leap.

Newly graduated and on my own, I worked in a few roles that left me longing for more intellectual stimulation (pharmaceutical competitive intelligence software sales, personal training, ACT prep tutoring). Although none were a perfect fit, each provided me with more insight into what I wanted from my future endeavors. I spent much of my free time searching for a path more closely aligned with what I wanted out of a career, and after much introspection and experimentation, it became clear that computer science could offer me a route to approach the complex problems I found interesting and cared about.

I enrolled in Northeastern’s ALIGN program – a graduate program designed to bridge non-CS majors to a master’s degree – and the experience has been as positive as I could have hoped. I enjoy writing code, coming up with new software ideas, and am always looking for new ways to apply my skills. At the time I was changing fields, my neuroscience background naturally catalyzed an interest in AI, although I only had a vague idea of what AI was. As I reach the end of my degree program, I’ve found myself (I suppose unsurprisingly) leaning into it; machine learning, classical AI methods, causal inference, and more experimental fronts like neurosymbolic reasoning.

I once again regularly contemplate pursuing a PhD, with a strong interest in AGI, but only time will tell.
